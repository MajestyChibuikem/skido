AI-Powered Lameness Detection in Cattle/Poultry
Background
Cattle farming plays an important role in food production and the livelihood of many farmers.
However, one common challenge farmers face is the difficulty in identifying when a cow is
developing lameness.
Lameness affects feeding, movement, growth, and milk production. Many farmers normally notice it
very late, when the cow is already limping badly.
Since farmers now use smartphones and basic digital tools, a simple web-based system that helps
them monitor their cows through video uploads can assist them in detecting signs of lameness
earlier and taking action quickly.
Problem Statement
Farmers often rely only on physical observation to detect lameness in cattle. Early signs are usually
very subtle and can be easily missed.
When lameness is noticed late, the cow's health worsens and the farmer spends more on treatment
or experiences economic loss.
There is no simple digital platform that helps farmers check their cattle's movement or keep records
of suspected lameness cases.
This project aims to solve this problem by providing a basic web-based system for monitoring and
reporting cattle movement.
Objectives
Main Objective:
To design and implement a simple web-based system that allows farmers to monitor cattle
movement and keep records of suspected lameness.
Specific Objectives:
- To create a web platform where farmers can upload short videos of their cattle.
- To store and organize movement records for future reference.
- To provide a simple interface that helps farmers compare current and past movement patterns.
- To help farmers make early decisions about treatment or veterinary consultation.
Main Focus
The main focus of this project is to provide farmers with a simple tool to detect lameness in their
cattle or poultry using videos. The process works as follows:
1. The farmer mounts cameras in their farm to record the movement of animals.
2. The farmer uploads short videos of the animals to the web-based platform.
3. The system automatically analyzes the videos and detects any signs of lameness.
4. Animals without any movement issues are marked as normal, while suspected lameness cases
are recorded for further attention.
This approach helps farmers monitor their livestock more efficiently, detect problems early, and
make timely decisions regarding treatment or veterinary care.
Methodology
The project will be developed using normal web technologies.
1. Frontend:
HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to create a simple and user-friendly interface.
Video upload page, dashboard, and history section.
2. Backend:
Python (Flask or Django) to handle form submissions and store uploaded videos.
Basic logic to allow farmers to label or describe what they observe.
3. Database:
SQLite or any simple database to store user details, videos, and notes.
4. Testing:
Use sample cattle videos to ensure upload, playback, and record management works properly.
Tools
Python (Flask/Django)
HTML / CSS / JavaScript
SQLite Database
Browser for testing
Expected Outcome
The system will provide farmers with:
- A simple platform to upload and save cow movement videos
- A digital record of suspected lameness cases
- A way to observe changes over time
- Better decision-making regarding treatment or veterinary attention
The end result is a basic but helpful tool that improves cattle health monitoring through simple
technology.
